Lasagna

Description

Lasagna (in Italian, lasagna) is a type of pasta. It's usually served in overlapping sheets interspersed with layers of ingredients of your choice, most often meat (ragu) in a Bolognese and béchamel sauce.

Ingredients

  • 600 g beef for stewing
  • 300 g pork belly
  • 1 onion
  • 2 carrots
  • 1 celery branch
  • 200 ml red wine
  • 800 g peeled tomatoes or tomato sauce
  • 50 ml milk
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • salt to taste
  • ground black pepper to taste

Bechamel

  • 80 g mantequilla
  • 80 g all-purpose flour
  • 750 ml leche
  • nutmeg to taste
  • salt to taste
  • black pepper to taste
  • 200 g Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. We cut the bacon into small squares. But first, we remove the skin because it's very tough.
  2. We put the bacon on medium heat so that it cooks slowly.
  3. Meanwhile, cut the vegetables into small squares.
  4. We didn't forget to stir the bacon!
  5. Add a splash of oil to the pan with the bacon, preferably olive oil.
  6. And we add the vegetables.
  7. We add 1 pinch of salt.
  8. And, over medium-low heat, we cook until the onion, carrot and celery soften.
  9. We add the beef.
  10. We make the heat medium high and stir very well.
  11. Once the meat has changed color, add 1 glass of red wine.
  12. We cook until the alcohol evaporates.
  13. Add tomato sauce or chopped tomatoes. I'm going to use whole canned tomatoes, but you can use any tomato sauce you like.
  14. Once the sauce has started to boil, we reduce the heat to low and put the lid on, leaving a gap for the steam to escape.
  15. The sauce should be cooked for 2 to 4 hours. Simmer very slowly. If it seems dry, add a little more water or broth.
  16. It took me 3 hours to cook the sauce.
  17. Season it to your liking.
  18. Add milk to balance the acidity.
  19. The sauce is ready.

Bechamel

  1. To make bechamel, melt the butter.
  2. Add the flour.
  3. Sauté the flour for 2-3 minutes over medium heat.
  4. Add the cold milk and turn the heat up to high.
  5. Stir very well. The flour dissolves very well in cold milk.
  6. The béchamel sauce must be stirred constantly until it thickens.
  7. As soon as it starts to boil, we reduce the heat to medium and cook for 2-3 more minutes.
  8. We remove the bechamel from the heat.
  9. And we season it. We add a pinch of nutmeg.
  10. Shall.
  11. And a little ground black pepper.
  12. To prevent a crust from forming, I cover it with a piece of plastic wrap.
  13. We grate the parmesan or parmigiano.
  14. Preheat oven to 180ºC or 360ºF with top and bottom heat.
  15. Grease the mold or baking dish with butter. My mold measures 24x24x6.5 cm.
  16. Cover the bottom with the bechamel.
  17. Place a layer of pasta on top.
  18. The ragù or meat sauce goes on top of the pasta.
  19. Drizzle the ragù with a little béchamel.
  20. Sprinkle with parmesan.
  21. Again we add pasta, ragù, béchamel, parmesan and so on until the mold is full.
  22. We generously cover the last layer with bechamel and sprinkle generously with cheese.
  23. We bake the lasagna for 45 minutes, the last 5 minutes on the grill at 250ºC.
  24. Before serving, let it rest for 10-15 minutes.
